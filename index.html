Dorchester Community Farm was established in 2010 as a project of Transition Town Dorchester, on land owned by the Duchy of Cornwall on the edge of Poundbury.

What began as a small growing project has grown into a thriving community space where people of all ages and backgrounds come to grow food, share skills, and build connection.

“The biggest changes that are going to happen are going to be at the local level.”

What the farm has to offer

From polytunnel to pond, orchard to wildlife habitat, the farm is a richly layered growing space designed to work with nature.

Permaculture growing

Raised beds, polytunnel, and forest garden cultivated using regenerative growing principles.

Heritage orchard

A community orchard of heritage apple and pear varieties, planted by local schoolchildren.

Beekeeping

Bee colonies maintained on site, supporting pollination across the farm and wider community.

Poultry & wildlife

Resident hens and geese roam the orchard, while the pond supports native biodiversity.

Off-grid aims

No mains water, rainwater harvesting, low-carbon aims, and practical sustainability throughout.

Forest garden

A developing woodland and forest garden area with plans for forest school and youth engagement.
